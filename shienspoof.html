Now let's get into the facts

Labor Practices

Shein Claims: “We have created the SHEIN Responsible Sourcing (SRS) evaluation system and conduct regular supplier audits. SHEIN's Responsible Sourcing Program assigns grades of A, B, C, D and Zero Tolerance Violations (ZTVs) to factories based on audit results. Examples of ZTVs include forced labor, serious environmental pollution, severe health and safety violations, and underage labor. SHEIN provides capacity building training to suppliers to support the remediation of ZTV and other major Code violations. Should suppliers fail to remediate violations within the allowed timeframe (immediately in severe cases, such as underage labor), we take further action, including termination.”

In Reality: In June 2022, videos circulating on social media claimed that Shein workers were embedding messages in the garments they manufactured, with one tag supposedly containing the plea "need your help" within the care instructions. Shein responded to these allegations through a TikTok video, asserting that it was a misunderstanding and emphasizing their "strict code of conduct for suppliers, which prohibits forced labor." According to Shein, the intention behind the "need your help" message was merely to prompt customers to use fabric softener when washing the garment to improve its texture. Despite the company's explanation, commenters remain skeptical, questioning the honesty of the company and urging the release of videos showcasing the actual working conditions instead of a brief explanatory video.

More Labor Practices

Shein Claims: "Our global workforce is nearly 10,000 strong — we value the hard work of every employee and implement policies to promote a workplace that is safe, secure and free from harassment and discrimination, and one that operates in compliance with relevant local laws."

In Reality: In October 2022, an undercover investigation by Channel 4 and The i newspaper in the UK uncovered that Shein employees work seven days a week, receiving a modest base salary of only $556 per month to produce 500 clothing items daily. This translates to a mere two cents per item produced. Some workers, particularly females, had to wash their hair during lunch breaks due to the lack of time after enduring 18-hour workdays.

Sustainablity

Shein Claims: “In an effort to reduce GHG emissions while enabling a seamless customer experience, SHEIN partners with last-mile delivery services providers, to offer order pickup services at local merchants. The last-mile shipping providers often use electric vehicles to drop packages at a central location — such as grocery stores, gas stations or salons — making retrieval convenient for customers while reducing the environmental impact that would be required to deliver every order to customers’ homes.”

In Reality: Most of Shein's products are dispatched from warehouses located in China, contributing significantly to a substantial carbon footprint. Presently, China stands as the world's largest emitter of greenhouse gasses, surpassing the combined emissions of all developed nations. Despite the United States being Shein's primary market, the company ships its products to a total of 220 countries worldwide.

Materials

Shein Claims: “Adam Whinston, the Global Head of ESG at Shein claims, “Sustainable material use is an area where I see a chance for growth, and I plan to incorporate more responsible fibers — such as recycled polyester — in SHEIN’s products. We’re also working on programs to reduce waste at product end-of-life, including partnerships to make garment recycling more accessible.”

In Reality: Out of the 52,000 dresses currently featured on the site, only 64 are reportedly crafted from recycled polyester. The starting price for these alternatives is $4, a notable contrast to sustainable brands where options rarely fall below $50. This pricing dynamic raises skepticism about the true eco-friendliness of these Shein options.

Packaging

Shein Claims: “Circular Economy and Product End-of-Life: SHEIN is currently developing innovative solutions and working with strategic partners to develop garment recycling programs and other initiatives to reduce post consumer waste.” The company also claims that they are “Striving for Zero Waste in Our Operations: We plan to announce a waste goal by the end of 2022 and have engaged with a leading waste management partner to help us achieve this goal.”

In Reality: The packaging materials used by Shein have become a topic of online conversation. Every item is individually enclosed in a plastic zip-top bag. Given the large volume of items shipped daily, this results in a considerable amount of plastic waste being directed to landfills.

Carbon Emissions & Waste Water

Shein Claims: “Digital Printing to Reduce Water Use: We look for innovative ways to reduce our environmental impact. We also invested in thermal digital transfer and digital direct printing technologies in an effort to reduce water use throughout the production process of SHEIN branded items.”

In Reality: The second most environmentally detrimental sector, surpassed only by the oil and gas industry, is fast fashion. This industry contributes to 20% of global wastewater and 10% of carbon emissions. The immense quantities of water and energy required for producing millions of pieces annually are undeniably substantial. Even if Shein has implemented or plans to implement rigorous measures to diminish waste and energy usage, achieving sustainability becomes implausible given the exceptionally high production rate. In essence, Shein is fundamentally unsustainable.
